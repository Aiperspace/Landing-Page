ECSS alignment you can prove.

ECSS standards use uniquely identified requirements to enable traceability and verification of compliance. When a requirement calls for a deliverable document, ECSS specifies the mandatory scope and content through a Document Requirements Definition (DRD)— and explicitly treats the content as mandatory, not the format. AIPER is built around that model: DRD-aware generation, cross-document consistency, and exportable evidence such as RTM and verification close-out traceability support (VCD).

ECSS tailoring results in documented applicability (EARM) and tracked compliance (ECM). AIPER supports producing and maintaining these artifacts as living outputs—not static spreadsheets.

Fits your toolchain.

AIPER integrates at the artifact level first: import/export DOCX, PDF, Markdown, CSV/XLSX, and structured traceability exports. For deeper workflows, AIPER exposes an API for syncing document entities, baselines, and releases.

FAQ

Short answers. Deep details in the demo.

How do you handle ECSS tailoring?

We support tailoring outputs (EARM) and compliance tracking (ECM) as structured, maintainable artifacts.

Do you lock us into a document format?

No—DRD content matters, not the form. We generate and validate content while preserving how your program packages deliverables.

Can we export an RTM and verification close-out evidence?

Yes—RTM export is supported, and verification close-out traceability is treated as a first-class output.

Is this configuration baseline friendly?

Yes—AIPER is designed around controlled baselines, diffs, and release artifacts.

Can we run on-prem?

Yes—deployment supports cloud, VPC, or on-prem patterns to meet program constraints.

Do you train AI models on our content?

Default posture: your data remains your data; training on your content requires explicit opt-in.
