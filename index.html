The real problem: baselines break before hardware does

Satellite programs don’t fail because teams can’t write. They fail because documentation baselines are fragmented: dependencies are hidden, traceability breaks, and release decisions happen without clear impact visibility.

Fragmented sources of truth

Engineering data is duplicated across documents, spreadsheets, and tools. Consistency becomes manual work.

Change propagation is unsafe

A small parameter change can affect multiple deliverables. Impact is difficult to see and easy to miss.

Broken traceability

Requirement-to-test-to-evidence links degrade over time. Reviews become evidence hunts.

Release friction and churn

Approvals, change logs, and version ping‑pong slow delivery. Engineering time goes into maintenance, not engineering.

FAQ

Short answers. We go deeper in the walkthrough.

Do you support ECSS and NASA documentation standards?

We support DRD-aligned structures and exportable evidence artifacts commonly used in ECSS and NASA-style documentation packages. Final sign-off remains with your engineering team.

Is this a replacement for our existing tools?

No. AIPER is designed to work alongside existing processes with standard exports. Selective integrations are available or on the roadmap depending on scope.

How do you handle change control?

AIPER tracks revisions, highlights change deltas, and supports controlled approvals so teams can review impacts before releasing a documentation package.

Can we run on‑prem or in a private environment?

Yes. Cloud, VPC, and on‑prem deployment options are available depending on program constraints.
